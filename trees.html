Trees

The Alphawood Arboretum

Did you know our very own campus here at Illinois Tech is considered a Level 1 accredited Arboretum. We have over 1,800 trees spanning about 70 different species. The addition of all these trees was thanks to landscape architect Alfred Caldwell. He worked in collaboration with the architect Mies who is the main architect of all the buildings on our campus, Caldwell's landscape design of irregular trees is truly what makes our campus unique. Some of the trees he planted included honey locust, gingko, and robinia, we will explore these next.

Types of Trees in The Alphawood Arboretum

  • Honey Locust

    Known to live up to 125 years, and they are great for creating shade, and withstanding harsh weather conditions. Perfect for Chicago's bipolar weather.

  • Gingko Trees

    This tree is able to grow in almost any condition, they are also able to live for thousands of year. A fun fact is that they have not changed in 200 million years, and are considered living fossils.

  • Robinia Tree

    Also known as the Black locust, it is very good at adapting different soils and can survive drought and harsh winters, again perfect for Chicago's climate. They can live anywehre from 60 to sometimes even a 100 years.
